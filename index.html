M A V A

We automated the recording of railway tickets at our railway station – via QR codes, countertop terminals and specialized cameras for QR scanning – we have seen many benefits: the productivity of our ticket booking office assistants has risen 30%, railway tickets rarely run out and our customer base has grown vigorously (according to our market research, this is at least partly due to the improved perception of professionalism and efficiency). The management feels that the Internet offers further exciting opportunities for increasing efficiency and reducing costs. For example, rather than printing out a catalog of what tickets are available, we could make the catalog available to every browser for browsing on-line. For privileged customers, we could provide extra railway purchasing services, such as railway trips at the click of a button. Our target saving in this area is a reduction of 18% in the cost of running each store. Within two years, using the full power of e-commerce, we aim to offer all our services via a Web browser, through delivery and pick-up at the customer’s home, thus achieving our ultimate goal of the virtual ticket booth company, with minimal running costs relative to walk-in train station booth.
